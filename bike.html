Bicycles: History, types and selection tips

Learn everything about bicycles - from the first inventions to modern models and useful tips.

Bicycle History

The bicycle was invented at the beginning of the XIX century. The first prototype was created by Karl Drais in 1817 - it was a wooden two-wheeled construction without pedals, which was called "running machine".

In 1839, Kirkpatrick Macmillan added pedals, and in the 1860s, metal frames appeared. The modern bicycle with chain drive was invented by John Kemp Starley in 1885.

Today bicycles are used for transportation, sports, tourism and active recreation all over the world.

Types of Bicycles

Mountain Bike (MTB)

Designed for riding on rough terrain. Has a strong frame, wide tires with tread and several speeds.

Road Bike

Optimized for riding on asphalt. Light construction, narrow tires, aerodynamic shape - ideal for high-speed riding.

City Bike

Convenient for daily trips around the city. Has an upright position, basket for things and chain protection.

Electric Bike

Equipped with an electric motor that assists with pedaling. Ideal for those who want to cover long distances.

Bicycle Selection Tips

  • Determine the purpose of use: What kind of trips do you plan to use the bicycle for?
  • Consider height and build: The correct frame size is the key to comfort and safety.
  • Check assembly quality: All components should be reliable and well-adjusted.
  • Test before purchase: Be sure to test ride the selected model.
  • Don't skimp on safety: Quality brakes and lighting are mandatory.

Remember that the right choice of bicycle guarantees comfort and enjoyment from riding!

Frequently Asked Questions

How to properly adjust seat height?

The seat should be set so that with the heel lowered on the pedal, the leg is slightly bent at the knee.

How often should the bicycle be serviced?

Regular maintenance includes cleaning after each ride, checking tire pressure and lubricating the chain every 500-1000 km.

Is it mandatory to use a helmet?

Yes, a helmet significantly reduces the risk of head injuries in case of a fall. This is a mandatory protective equipment for all cyclists.
